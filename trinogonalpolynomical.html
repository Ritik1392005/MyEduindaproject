Polynomial

Definition

A Polynomial is an algebraic expression made up of variables, constants, and exponents combined using addition, subtraction, and multiplication.

The exponent of the variable in a polynomial must always be a whole number (0, 1, 2, 3, …).

General Form of Polynomial

P(x)=aₙxⁿ+aₙ₋₁xⁿ⁻¹+aₙ₋₂xⁿ⁻²+...+a₁x+a₀
  • aₙ, aₙ₋₁, a₀ are constants
  • x is the variable
  • n is a non-negative integer

Examples of Polynomial

1. x² + 5x + 6

2. 3x³ - 2x + 7

3. 5x⁴ + 3x² + 9

Non-Polynomial Examples

The following are not polynomials because exponents are negative, fractional, or variables are in denominator.

1. 1/x + 2

2. x⁻² + 3

3. √x + 5

Types of Polynomial Based on Number of Terms

1. Monomial

A polynomial having one term.

5x²

2. Binomial

A polynomial having two terms.

x² + 3

3. Trinomial

A polynomial having three terms.

x² + 5x + 6

Types of Polynomial Based on Degree

Degree Name Example
0 Constant Polynomial 5
1 Linear Polynomial 2x + 3
2 Quadratic Polynomial x² + 4x + 4
3 Cubic Polynomial x³ + 2x² + 5
4 Quartic Polynomial x⁴ + x + 1

Degree of Polynomial

The highest exponent of the variable in a polynomial is called its degree.

Polynomial Degree
5x + 3 1
x² + 7x + 10 2
4x⁵ + x² + 1 5

Polynomial Operations

1. Addition of Polynomial

(2x² + 3x + 1) + (x² + 5x + 4)

= 3x² + 8x + 5

2. Subtraction of Polynomial

(5x² + 7x + 9) - (2x² + 3x + 4)

= 3x² + 4x + 5

3. Multiplication of Polynomial

(x + 2)(x + 3)

= x² + 3x + 2x + 6

= x² + 5x + 6

Polynomial Identities

1. Square of Sum

(a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
(x+3)² = x² + 6x + 9

2. Square of Difference

(a-b)² = a² - 2ab + b²
(x-2)² = x² - 4x + 4

3. Difference of Squares

a² - b² = (a+b)(a-b)
x² - 25 = (x+5)(x-5)

Zeros of Polynomial

The value of variable for which polynomial becomes zero is called a zero/root of the polynomial.

x² - 5x + 6 = 0

(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0

Roots: x = 2, x = 3

Factorization of Polynomial

Writing a polynomial as multiplication of simpler polynomials is called factorization.

x² + 7x + 12

= (x + 3)(x + 4)

Remainder Theorem

If a polynomial P(x) is divided by (x-a), then the remainder is P(a).

P(x)=x²+3x+2

P(1)=1²+3(1)+2

= 1+3+2

= 6
